# World Cup Tournament Format Reference

## Current Format (2026 onward, 48 teams)

- **Group stage**: 12 groups of 4 teams (A–L). Each team plays 3 matches.
- **Advancement**: Top 2 in each group + 8 best third-placed teams advance to Round of 32.
- **Knockout stage**: single-elimination from Round of 32 to final.
- **Knockout ties**: 90 minutes; if level, 30 minutes extra time; if still level, penalty shoot-out.

## Legacy Format (1998–2022, 32 teams)

- 8 groups of 4 teams (A–H).
- Top 2 advance to Round of 16.
- Same knockout tie rules.

## Group-Stage Tie-Breakers (in order)

1. Points obtained in all group matches.
2. Goal difference in all group matches.
3. Goals scored in all group matches.
4. Points obtained in head-to-head matches among tied teams.
5. Goal difference in head-to-head matches among tied teams.
6. Goals scored in head-to-head matches among tied teams.
7. Fair play points (yellow/red cards).
8. Drawing of lots by FIFA.

If only two teams are tied after step 3, steps 4–6 apply directly. If more than two are tied and one is separated by 4–6, restart at step 1 for the remaining teams.

## Third-Place Ranking (when applicable)

For 24-team and 48-team formats, best third-placed teams advance. Ranking criteria:

1. Points.
2. Goal difference.
3. Goals scored.
4. Wins.
5. Fair play points.
6. Drawing of lots.

## Knockout Bracket Mapping

For the 48-team 2026 format, bracket slots are pre-defined by group positions. A useful simplification:

- Round of 32 pairings depend on which third-place groups qualify.
- Round of 16 onwards has fixed paths once the bracket is known.

When projecting a bracket, always state the assumed third-place qualifiers.
